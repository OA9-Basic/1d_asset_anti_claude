 ● When users contribute more than $1 (e.g., $60 for a $100 course), should they get refunded the excess immediately when the asset is fully funded, or should they get store credit/rewards?
   → If an amount like 60 out of 100 is sent for any asset, the following will happen: When the target amount for the asset is reached (100 + the platform's profit margin), the asset will be purchased and
   distributed to everyone. When the asset is purchased again, some of the profits will be allocated to each over-contributor according to their contribution. For example, one person sent 60 and another sent
   10. On that day, we received 30 additional purchases of the asset worth $1 each. If our profit is $30, we keep a profit margin, and the remaining profits are distributed between the two people, with the
   larger share going to the person with the larger contribution. This process continues daily until all the money has been sent to the over-contributors. Remember that the money sent to the over-contributors
   is the excess profit generated from the asset purchased with the initial $100 contribution. This asset is held on the platform and always sold for its original contribution amount of $1. The money returned
   to them will not be Store Credit. Instead, they will have a withdrawable amount in their wallet that can also be converted to Store Credit. The site's mechanism will make them highly addicted to the site,
   encouraging them to convert it to Store Credit without realizing it, thus increasing the site's profitability.
